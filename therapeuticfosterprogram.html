THERAPEUTIC FOSTER PROGRAM

When our shelter has reached capacity, or we get an animal that needs special care and attention before being eligible for adoption, we utilize our Therapeutic Foster Program. The animals that are part of our foster program are placed with a specially trained volunteer in an approved environment for a temporary period of time. The volunteer provides love and care for the animal throughout the duration of their placement while we work to find them a forever home. These animals will typically go straight from the foster environment to a forever home and don’t spend time in our shelter. This is a win-win, because it provides a safe haven for the animal and also allows for room at the shelter for us to save more animals on euthanasia lists at local kill-shelters.


A typical placement usually lasts between four and twelve weeks. Our fur friends that spend time in the foster program are generally newborn kittens that require bottle feeding and round the clock care, animals that have lost their elderly companions and would not adjust well in a shelter setting, and animals that are in recovery or have experienced trauma.


Becoming a foster is an incredibly rewarding experience and one that we encourage. Many of our fosters have even fallen in love with their foster babies and ended up adopting.

Not able to volunteer time, but still want to show your support?
Please consider making a donation.

Our volunteers are provided with items necessary for proper care throughout the duration of their placement. Items may be syringes, bottles, crates, toys, special food, or medication. Without the support of our generous donors, this would not be possible. Donations allow us to provide for our animals and ensure they get quality care during their placement.
